10 Ways you can use a Cow Print in your Crafts

Inspiration

Cow print is a classic, versatile pattern that can add a rustic, farmhouse-inspired look to all kinds of craft projects. Here are 10 ideas for incorporating cow print into your handmade creations:

  1. Throw Pillows: Sew up some cozy throw pillows using cow print fabric for an easy way to add a touch of country chic to your living room or bedroom.
  2. Tote Bags: Make a stylish and practical tote bag with cow print cotton or canvas fabric. This makes a great reusable shopping bag or everyday carryall.
  3. Coasters: Cut out cow print fabric circles or squares to make a set of absorbent drink coasters. Seal with a clear sealant for easy cleaning.
  4. Bulletin Board: Cover a plain cork bulletin board with cow print fabric for an eye-catching way to display notes, photos, and reminders.
  5. Wreaths: Wrap cow print ribbon around a grapevine or straw wreath form for a farm-inspired decoration.
  6. Placemats: Sew up a set of reversible placemats using cow print on one side and a coordinating solid or stripe on the other.
  7. Lampshades: Recover a plain lampshade with cow print fabric for a quick decor update.
  8. Jewelry Trays: Decoupage cow print paper or fabric onto small wooden trays or boxes to create cute catchalls for earrings, rings, and other jewelry.
  9. Tissue Box Covers: Wrap a cow print fabric panel around a basic tissue box for a stylish accent.
  10. Jar Labels: Print out cow print labels and adhere them to canning jars, recycled glass containers, or other home organization pieces.

The versatility of cow print makes it easy to incorporate into all kinds of craft projects, from home decor to accessories and more. Get creative and add a touch of rustic charm with this classic farmhouse pattern.

Versatility of Cow Print

Cow print, also known as cowhide or Holstein print, is a versatile pattern that works well in a variety of craft projects and home decor styles. The classic black and white or brown and white spotted print has a timeless, farmhouse-inspired look that can create a rustic, country feel. But cow print can also be used in more modern, minimalist designs for a contemporary twist.

The print's neutral color palette makes it easy to pair with solid colors, stripes, plaids, and other patterns to create visually interesting designs. Cow print can be the star of the show or used as an accent to complement other elements.

Where to Find Cow Print Supplies

When sourcing cow print materials for your crafts, you have lots of options:

  • Fabric stores - Look for cow print in the cotton, canvas, or home decor fabric sections. You can find it in yardage as well as pre-made items like throw pillows and tote bags.
  • Craft stores - Many craft supply retailers carry cow print paper, vinyl, washi tape, and other craft-specific materials.
  • Online - Platforms like Etsy, Amazon, and specialty fabric sites offer a wide selection of cow print fabrics, papers, and other cow print craft supplies.
  • Upcycle - Re-purpose and upcycle existing items like place mats, dishtowels, or clothing with a cow print pattern.

Craft Project Ideas
In addition to the 10 ideas covered earlier, here are some more ways you can use cow print in your crafts:

  • Reusable grocery/produce bags
  • Framed wall art or gallery wall
  • Decorative garland or bunting
  • Sewing machine cover
  • Makeup brush holder
  • Keychains or luggage tags
  • Trinket dishes or jewelry trays
  • Scrapbook albums or memory books
  • Painted accent furniture pieces
  • Handmade cards and stationery

The key is to let your creativity shine and find ways to incorporate this classic farmhouse print into projects that fit your personal style and home decor aesthetic. Cow print is a versatile, timeless pattern that can work for a wide range of crafters.
